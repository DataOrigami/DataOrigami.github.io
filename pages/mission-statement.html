Mission Statement

There are an infinite ways to fold paper, but only an a fraction of ways to make something meaningful, let alone attractive. Data behaves in the same way: the space of possible folds on data is infinite, and exploring that space to find meaningful folds is difficult. The Data Origami group finds the folding lines of data, and make the pattern as transparent as possible.

Many public groups have enourmous amounts of data, and lack ideas on how to use it. We work with these groups to answer their questions and show them the questions they haven't even thought yet. Our first questions is asking "What needs to change for you to be helped?"

Who are our members

The group is open to all members of the community, independent of any technical talent.

What is our long-term goal?

Chaotic Good. This can be in two forms:

  1. Provide public groups with tools and information to benefit their community.
  2. Create attention-grabbing data insight into Canada and, particularly, Ottawa's community.

We want the Primer Minister of Canada to ask "Who's Data Origami?". We want journalist to cite our results. We want citizens to think "Ah, this is a Data Origami job."
